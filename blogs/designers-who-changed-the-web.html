Beginner Fitness: The Path to a Healthier You

The digital world has made everything, from the smallest to the largest detail, convenient and comfortable for human beings. There’s no need to do laundry by hand; we have washing machines and dryers. We don’t have to fetch water from wells; it is delivered via motors.

July 28, 2022
·
6 min

Eventually, somewhere along the way, we went from active, physically hardworking humans to laid-back people who take cars to the convenience store by the street corner and lack fitness. And proportionally, with less physical activity, human health has steadily depleted with diseases like diabetes, high blood pressure, etc.

According to the WHO, 43% of adults are overweight. To put it in simple terms, every 2 in 5 adults is overweight.

Although people worry about the aesthetics of being overweight, the real threats are the elements that affect the quality of life, along with a myriad of diseases and disorders.

Hence, maintaining healthy fitness levels is important.

In this blog, let’s decipher how you can lead a healthy life.

Importance of Fitness

Fitness is not just adults hitting the gym and lifting heavy weights. Fitness is important for people of all ages—kids, adults, and the elderly, and it can be in any form. It could be comfortable trekking, calming yoga, gardening, dancing, Pilates, rock climbing, and a hundred other options.

The prime importance of fitness is that it prevents the onset of many diseases and helps manage a lot of other diseases. With a healthy lifestyle and regular fitness activities, it is possible to avoid heart disease and diabetes and maintain hormonal conditions like PCOD. Having ideal levels of fitness ensures respiratory health, cardiovascular health, and a healthy weight.

Exercise has also proved to be a factor that contributes to increased life expectancy. People who are active have a reduced risk of injuries, have better mental health, and tend to live longer.

When it comes to reducing injury, fitness makes it possible by increasing muscle mass, bone density, flexibility, and stability.

There’s a direct relationship between mental health and fitness. Dopamine increases during workouts, and you are bound to feel happy and satisfied after a workout. Furthermore, exercising in general contributes to better health through dedication, consistency, celebrating small successes, belonging to a community, and the aesthetics of your body.

Setting goals for the fitness journey

Fitness is not a goal but a journey to be lived, repeated, and enjoyed. So, it is important to not be caught up in the vortex of numbers but instead, define goals by achievements and progress.

To set your goals, first, you must realize your ultimate goal to be achieved. It can be being active, gaining six-pack abs, or recovering from an injury. So, define your goal and write it down on paper. Experts also agree that it’s better to have a singular, achievable goal than to have multiple goals, which might cause frustration and confusion.

Being realistic is an essential element of goal-setting. You can’t just aspire to lose 10 kg in 10 days, which is not possible and sustainable in the long run. But what you can do is lose 10 kg in a 10-day period with moderate effort. So be realistic about your goals and consider your circumstances, routines, habits, and demographics before committing to your fitness journey.

Setting goals for the fitness journey

  • Be specific
  • Set small, attainable goals.
  • Consistently monitor your habits.
  • Be adaptable
  • Take it slow.
  • Listen to your mind and body.
